Detailed Phase Breakdown

Phase 1

Requirements Definition

Government defines what they need:

  • Market Research (what's available?)
  • PWS/SOW Development (scope of work)
  • IGCE (cost estimate)
  • Acquisition Strategy
  • Set-aside determination (small business?)
Phase 2

Sources Sought / RFI

Government gauges industry interest:

  • Posted on SAM.gov
  • Capability statements requested
  • Industry feedback on requirements
  • Small business interest assessment
Phase 3

Draft RFP / Industry Day

Government refines requirements with industry:

  • Draft solicitation released
  • Industry Day Q&A session
  • Written questions/answers
  • Teaming partner identification
Phase 4

Final RFP Release

Official solicitation with all details:

  • Performance Work Statement (PWS)
  • Evaluation Criteria (LPTA, Best Value, etc.)
  • Contract Line Items (CLINs)
  • Labor Categories (LCATs)
  • Proposal instructions
Phase 5-6

Proposal & Evaluation

Competition plays out:

  • Technical Volume (approach, past perf)
  • Management Volume (org, key personnel)
  • Price/Cost Volume
  • Government evaluation team scoring
  • Discussions/BAFO (if applicable)
Phase 7-9

Award & Kickoff

Winner announced and work begins:

  • Award notification to all offerors
  • Debrief requests
  • GAO protest window (10 days)
  • Court of Federal Claims (longer)
  • Transition planning (incumbent to new)
  • Contract kickoff meeting

Contract Vehicle Types
IDIQ

Indefinite Delivery / Indefinite Quantity

Most common for large DoD programs. Pre-negotiated terms, task orders issued as needed.

  • Single-Award IDIQ: One contractor, multiple task orders
  • Multi-Award IDIQ (MAC): Multiple contractors compete for each TO
  • Base + option years (typically 5-10 year ceiling)
  • Minimum/maximum order values
BPA

Blanket Purchase Agreement

Simplified ordering for recurring needs. Often used under GSA schedules.

  • Pre-negotiated pricing
  • Simplified ordering procedures
  • Single or multiple award
  • Lower ceiling values than IDIQ
GSA

GSA Schedule (MAS)

Pre-approved vendors with negotiated rates. Agencies order directly without full competition.

  • IT Schedule 70 (now consolidated)
  • Professional Services Schedule
  • Pre-competed pricing
  • Faster procurement
GWACs

Government-Wide Acquisition Contracts

Large umbrella contracts available to all federal agencies:

  • SEWP V: NASA IT products/services
  • Alliant 2: GSA IT services
  • CIO-SP3: NIH IT services
  • 8(a) STARS III: Small business IT
FFP

Firm Fixed Price

Contractor bears cost risk. Price set regardless of actual costs incurred.

  • Best for well-defined requirements
  • Government pays agreed price
  • Contractor incentive to control costs
  • Less government oversight
T&M / LH

Time & Materials / Labor Hour

Government pays for hours worked. Common for services with uncertain scope.

  • Hourly rates by labor category
  • Government bears more cost risk
  • Requires more oversight
  • Ceiling price (not-to-exceed)
CPFF

Cost Plus Fixed Fee

Government reimburses costs plus negotiated fee. For R&D and uncertain scope.

  • All allowable costs reimbursed
  • Fixed fee percentage
  • Heavy audit requirements
  • Less common for IT services
OTAS

Other Transaction Agreements

Flexible agreements outside FAR. Used for prototyping and innovation.

  • Not subject to FAR/DFARS
  • Faster, more flexible
  • Prototype OTs can lead to production
  • DIU, AFWERX, SOFWERX

üéØ Where PTS Fits

As an SDVOSB staffing company, PTS typically enters the supply chain as a Tier 2-3 subcontractor to large primes (GDIT, BAE, Leidos). The prime holds the IDIQ or BPA, then flows down staffing requirements to PTS through subcontract agreements. PTS can also pursue direct contracts through small business set-asides (8(a), SDVOSB sole-source up to $5M) or GSA Schedule ordering.

Money Flow

How Payment Works

  • Congress ‚Üí Air Force (appropriations)
  • Air Force ‚Üí BAE (prime payment)
  • BAE ‚Üí GDIT (sub payment)
  • GDIT ‚Üí PTS (sub-sub payment)

Acronym Glossary
IDIQ Indefinite Delivery/Indefinite Quantity
Contract type with a ceiling value but no guaranteed minimum. Task orders issued as work is needed. Most common for large DoD IT programs.
TO Task Order
Specific work package issued under an IDIQ. Can be for a site, capability, or time-limited surge. Each TO has its own funding and scope.
PWS Performance Work Statement
Document describing what the contractor must accomplish (outcomes). Focus on results rather than how to do the work.
SOW Statement of Work
Document describing how work should be done (prescriptive). More detailed than PWS, specifies methods and processes.
LCAT Labor Category
Defined job roles with minimum qualifications, experience requirements, and billing rates. E.g., "Network Engineer III" or "Sr. Cyber Analyst".
CLIN Contract Line Item Number
Specific priced item in a contract. Each CLIN has its own funding, pricing, and tracking. CLINs organize contract deliverables.
CO/KO Contracting Officer
Government official with authority to enter, modify, and terminate contracts. Only person who can legally bind the government.
COR Contracting Officer's Representative
Government employee designated to monitor contractor performance. Technical expert who interfaces with contractor daily.
PEO Program Executive Officer
Senior government official responsible for a portfolio of related programs. Reports to service acquisition executive.
IGCE Independent Government Cost Estimate
Government's internal estimate of what a contract should cost. Used to evaluate proposals and ensure fair pricing.
BAFO Best and Final Offer
Final proposal revision submitted after discussions. Government requests BAFOs when negotiations reach conclusion.
LPTA Lowest Price Technically Acceptable
Evaluation method where lowest price wins among technically acceptable proposals. No tradeoffs ‚Äî meet requirements at lowest cost.
SDVOSB Service-Disabled Veteran-Owned Small Business
Socioeconomic category for businesses owned 51%+ by service-disabled veterans. Eligible for set-asides and sole-source contracts up to $5M.
PoP Period of Performance
Time frame during which contract work is performed. Typically structured as base year plus option years (e.g., 1+4).
FFP Firm Fixed Price
Contract type with set price regardless of costs. Contractor bears cost risk. Best for well-defined requirements.
T&M Time and Materials
Contract type paying for hours worked at fixed rates. Government bears more risk. Common for services with uncertain scope.
OTA Other Transaction Agreement
Flexible agreement outside traditional FAR rules. Used for prototyping and innovation. Faster and more flexible than traditional contracts.
GAO Government Accountability Office
Congressional agency that hears bid protests. Losing offerors can protest within 10 days of award. GAO decision in ~100 days.
